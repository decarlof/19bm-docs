=====
About
=====

The **19-BM Fast Autonomous Computed Tomography (FACT)** beamline is
a dedicated, autonomous, high-throughput X-ray computed-tomography
beamline at the Advanced Photon Source (APS), Argonne National
Laboratory.

Scientific scope
----------------

FACT focuses on high-throughput, autonomous computed tomography of
a wide range of user samples using **filtered white-beam tomography**.
Indirect-detection systems provide 3-D images at micron resolution.
With robotic sample manipulation, a high scan cadence is supported,
enabling sample-throughput regimes not previously available at the
APS imaging beamlines.

Beamline at a glance
--------------------

:Sector: 19-BM, APS
:Source: APS bending-magnet (BM)
:Energy mode: filtered white beam only (no monochromator)
:Horizontal acceptance: 0.8 mrad
:Centerline offset: 0.4 mrad inboard of the default new BM centerline
:Enclosures: 19-BM-A (FOE), 19-BM-C (transport), 19-BM-D (endstation)
:First light: target 2026-3 beam cycle
:Beamline scientist: Alan Kastengren (IMG/XSD)
:Group leader: Francesco DeCarlo (IMG/XSD)

Status
------

As of the FDR (Final Design Report, 6 June 2026), removal of legacy
19-BM equipment is ongoing. First light in the new beamline
configuration is anticipated during the 2026-3 beam cycle.
